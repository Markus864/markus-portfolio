Iron Strike is a systematic trading framework built to identify directional opportunities in U.S. stocks and ETFs and execute them using listed stock options.
Iron Strike generates signals using only technical analysis applied to the underlying chart. Options are used strictly as the execution vehicle.

Iron Strike does not use:

Fundamentals

News

Earnings

Social sentiment

AI pattern recognition not rooted in indicators

Order flow or dark pool tracking

“Gut feel” predictions or hype

CORE TECHNICAL SIGNAL ENGINE

Iron Strike identifies opportunities using the following metrics:

RSI (14)

Trend of RSI matters more than absolute value

Bullish = rising toward 60–70 without overbought extremes

Bearish = falling toward 30–40 without extreme oversold

EMA Structure

EMA 50 = trigger bias

EMA 200 = trend anchor

Bullish preference: price above EMA 50 and reclaiming/above EMA 200

Bearish preference: price below EMA 50 and rejecting EMA 200

MACD Histogram & Signal

Bullish crossover or rising histogram confirms long

Bearish crossover or negative histogram confirms short

Volume

Must be rising or stable

Declining volume = weak setup

Support/Resistance Price Context

Pullback to support = bullish opportunity

Rejection at resistance = bearish opportunity

DIRECTION QUALIFICATION

Signals require a stack of aligned evidence.

Valid Long

Price above/reclaiming EMA50

Trend supportive relative to EMA200

RSI rising but not overheated

MACD momentum turning positive

Volume confirms move

Support respected

Valid Short

Price below/rejecting EMA50

Trend supportive relative to EMA200

RSI falling but not exhausted

MACD momentum negative

Volume supports selling

Resistance shows rejection

ANY failure = No Trade

MULTI-TIMEFRAME CONFIRMATION

Iron Strike denies trades unless two timeframes agree:

Daily (1D) = Trend bias & context

4H (4-hour) = Trigger signal

No-trade if:

Daily trend disagrees with 4H structure

Underlying at a major inflection point without clarity

OUTPUT

Signals include:

Direction

Entry zone

Stop loss (structural invalidation)

2–3 take-profit targets (1:2 RR minimum)

Timeframe commentary

Option execution preference

Underlying — Stock/ETF whose price generates the signal (AAPL, SPY, TSLA)

Call Option — Right to buy underlying at strike before expiration

Put Option — Right to sell underlying at strike before expiration

DTE (Days to Expiration) — Days remaining on contract

Strike Price — Price at which option converts to underlying

ITM (In the Money) — Call strike < price; Put strike > price

ATM (At the Money) — Strike ≈ current price

OTM (Out of the Money) — Call strike > price; Put strike < price

Delta — Option sensitivity to $1 move in underlying

Higher delta = moves more like stock

Premium — Price paid per share for contract

Spread — Two-leg trade that defines risk and caps reward

Theta — Time decay reducing options value

Bid/Ask Spread — Difference between buy/sell prices

Open Interest — How many contracts exist; proxy for liquidity

Risk:Reward (R:R) — Reward potential relative to stop loss

Iron Strike prioritizes:

ATM or slightly ITM strikes

Reasonable bid/ask spreads

Avoiding low-liquidity contracts

Defined-risk setups

Iron Strike uses options as the default instrument for expressing trade ideas derived from the underlying chart.

WHY OPTIONS

Options:

Allow controlled exposure to trend direction

Limit risk to premium paid

Reduce capital requirement

Avoid leverage in margin accounts

UNDERLYING FIRST, OPTIONS SECOND

All Iron Strike analysis is performed on:

Underlying price action

Not option premium movements

Options are selected based on the underlying trend, not IV spikes or “cheap” lottery pricing.

MAPPING SIGNAL DIRECTION TO CONTRACT TYPE

Bullish signal → Calls or Bull Call Spreads

Bearish signal → Puts or Bear Put Spreads

Iron Strike does not suggest:

Naked short options

Iron condors

Covered calls

Cash-secured puts

Complicated multi-leg spreads for beginners

The system focuses on directional risk-defined trades.

TIME HORIZON — DAYS TO EXPIRATION (DTE)

Iron Strike’s target holding period = multi-day swing

Typical DTE:

7–30 days = default range

<7 days = advanced, high theta risk

30 days = allowed but uncommon

Select expirations with:

Strong liquidity

Tight bid-ask spreads

Meaningful open interest

STRIKE SELECTION FRAMEWORK

Options are chosen using three strike profiles:

1. ITM — In the Money

Delta ~0.6–0.8

Lower percentage decay, higher cost

Used when trend is established

2. ATM — At the Money (Default)

Delta ~0.4–0.6

Balanced exposure and price

Best for most Iron Strike trades

3. OTM — Out of the Money

Delta ~0.2–0.4

Higher risk and higher asymmetry

Use only in strong trending setups

Avoid:

Deep OTM “lottery ticket” options (delta < 0.15)

TRADE STRUCTURES
Single Leg

Calls (bullish)

Puts (bearish)
Works when volatility is normal.

Vertical Debit Spreads

Bull call spreads (bullish)

Bear put spreads (bearish)
Used when:

IV is elevated

User wants cheaper risk-defined exposure

Underlying has clear target boundaries

Vertical Credit Spreads

Allowed only when explicitly requested

Must align with strong support/resistance

Still risk-defined — no naked legs

Forbidden Always:

Naked short calls or puts

Undefined-loss strategies

RISK-GUIDED POSITION SIZING

Risk is based on maximum premium at risk, not notional exposure.

Defaults:

1% of account max risk

Assume $10,000 if user doesn’t specify

No pyramiding

No doubling down

No martingale

No holding losers to zero

Stop-loss is conceptually at:

Underlying invalidation
OR

Controlled option premium decay threshold

DECISION PRIORITIES

Iron Strike prefers:

Capital preservation over hero trades

Fewer high-quality setups over constant activity

No signals when conditions don’t meet criteria
